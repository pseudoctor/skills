## 0) Paper ID

## 1) Problem & Motivation

- **一句话问题定义**: Whether the traditional analysis of knowledge as justified true belief (JTB) is correct
  - **证据摘录**: "I shall begin by noting two points... That in that sense of 'justified' in which S's being justified in believing P is a necessary condition of S's knowing that P, it is possible for a person to be justified in believing a proposition that is in fact false"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, para.2

- **为什么重要**: The JTB analysis has been widely accepted in epistemology, so showing it's inadequate would be philosophically significant
  - **证据摘录**: "Plato seems to be considering some such definition [of knowledge]... and Chisholm has lately suggested that the following gives the necessary and sufficient conditions for knowledge"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, para.1

---

## 2) Key Contribution / Central Claim

- **核心主张**: Justified true belief is NOT sufficient for knowledge - one can have a justified true belief that still fails to count as knowledge
  - **证据摘录**: "I shall argue that (a) is false in that the conditions stated therein do not constitute a sufficient condition for the truth of the proposition that S knows that P"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, para.1

- **与既有观点区分**: Contra traditional JTB analysis defended by philosophers from Plato to contemporary epistemologists like Chisholm
  - **证据摘录**: "Various attempts have been made... to state necessary and sufficient conditions for someone's knowing a given proposition... Chisholm has lately suggested that the following gives the necessary and sufficient conditions"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, para.1

---

## 3) Key Concepts

- **术语1**: Justified belief (有根据的信念)
  - **定义**: S is justified in believing P if S has adequate evidence/reasons for P, regardless of P's truth value
  - **证据摘录**: "In that sense of 'justified' in which S's being justified in believing P is a necessary condition of S's knowing that P, it is possible for a person to be justified in believing a proposition that is in fact false"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, para.2, Point (i)

- **术语2**: Entailment principle (蕴含原则)
  - **定义**: If S is justified in believing P, and P entails Q, and S deduces Q from P and accepts Q, then S is justified in believing Q
  - **证据摘录**: "For any proposition P, if S is justified in believing P, and P entails Q, and S deduces Q from P and accepts Q as a result of this deduction, then S is justified in believing Q"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, para.2, Point (ii)

---

## 4) Distinctions & Framework

- **区分1**: Knowledge vs. Justified True Belief
  - **作用**: Central to the argument - showing these come apart in Gettier cases
  - **证据摘录**: "These two examples show that definition (a) [JTB] does not state a sufficient condition for someone's knowing a given proposition"
  - **位置标记**: p.122, final para

- **区分2**: Justification vs. Truth
  - **作用**: Allows for justified false beliefs, which is crucial for constructing counterexamples
  - **证据摘录**: "It is possible for a person to be justified in believing a proposition that is in fact false"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, Point (i)

---

## 5) Argument Skeleton

### 论证链条:

- **P1**: The JTB account claims: S knows P iff (i) P is true, (ii) S believes P, (iii) S is justified in believing P
  - **证据摘录**: "S knows that P IFF (i) P is true, (ii) S believes that P, and (iii) S is justified in believing that P"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, condition (a)

- **P2**: It's possible to be justified in believing false propositions
  - **证据摘录**: "It is possible for a person to be justified in believing a proposition that is in fact false"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, Point (i)

- **P3**: Justification is preserved under deduction (entailment principle)
  - **证据摘录**: "For any proposition P, if S is justified in believing P, and P entails Q... then S is justified in believing Q"
  - **位置标记**: p.121, Point (ii)

- **I1**: Therefore, we can construct cases where: (a) S justifiedly believes a false P, (b) S deduces true Q from P, (c) S has justified true belief in Q
  - **证据摘录**: Case I (Smith/Jones/10 coins) and Case II (Smith/Barcelona) demonstrate this structure
  - **位置标记**: p.122, Cases I & II

- **I2**: In these cases, S has JTB in Q but intuitively lacks knowledge of Q (because the justification traces through a false belief)
  - **证据摘录**: 【原文未明确说"缺乏知识"，但通过反例结构隐含这一判断】
  - **位置标记**: Implicit in case construction

- **C**: Therefore, JTB is not sufficient for knowledge
  - **证据摘录**: "These two examples show that definition (a) does not state a sufficient condition for someone's knowing a given proposition"
  - **位置标记**: p.122, conclusion

### 隐含前提检查:
- **隐含桥梁1**: In the Gettier cases, Q is true only by "luck" (different fact than the one justifying belief)
  - **状态**: 【原文未提供桥梁论证 - 未明确说为何这些案例中的JTB不算知识】
  - The paper relies on reader's intuition that these are not knowledge cases

- **隐含桥梁2**: Knowledge requires proper causal/evidential connection, not just JTB
  - **状态**: 【原文未明确陈述积极理论 - 仅提供反例，未说什么应该加入JTB】

### 反例/思想实验:
- **Case I (Smith and Jones)**: Smith believes "The man who will get the job has 10 coins" based on justified false belief that Jones will get the job; turns out Smith himself gets job and has 10 coins
  - **证据摘录**: "Imagine that Smith and Jones have applied for a certain job... Smith has strong evidence for the following conjunctive proposition: (d) Jones is the man who will get the job, and Jones has ten coins in his pocket"
  - **位置标记**: p.122, Case I
  - **作用**: Challenges sufficiency of condition (iii) in JTB definition

- **Case II (Barcelona)**: Smith believes "Either Jones owns a Ford, or Brown is in Barcelona" based on justified false Ford belief; turns out Brown really is in Barcelona (by luck)
  - **证据摘录**: "Let us suppose that Smith has strong evidence for the following proposition: (f) Jones owns a Ford... Imagine that Brown, of whom Smith has no idea where he is, is in fact in Barcelona"
  - **位置标记**: p.122, Case II
  - **作用**: Shows same problem with disjunctive beliefs

  - **位置标记**: p.121, Point (ii)

  - **位置标记**: p.122, Cases I & II
